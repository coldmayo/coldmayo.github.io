Why I love Klaud

and why he's the greatest Star Wars character ever


Who is Klaud

Klaud is a Star Wars character who's first appearance was in Episode IX - The Rise of Skywalker. He's a male Trodatome from a planet at the edge of Hutt Space called Troda. He always had an affinity for engineering and mechanics. His life would change when he came into contact with Chewbacca who convinced him to join the Resistance as a mechanic. He would then accompany them on various missions and would gain a good reputuation from the likes of Princess Leia Organa and Finn. As a result of his popularity, he was included in Star Wars: Skywalker Saga and other characters of his species were put in other pieces of media.


Representation

Klaud's presence adds a layer of diversity to the Star Wars universe. As an alien species hailing from the mostly unknown planet Troda, Klaud showcases the variety of life forms and cultures that exist within the galaxy. His unique appearance and background contribute to the overarching theme of unity in the face of oppression, as various species come together to fight against the First Order.


Unlikely Hero

Klaud embodies the concept of an unsung hero, someone who works diligently behind the scenes to support the main heroes of the story. While he might not receive the same level of attention as characters like Rey or Kylo Ren, his contributions are no less significant. This reflects the real world, where countless individuals contribute to the greater good without seeking recognition.


Conclusions

In conclusion, Klaud's greatness lies in his ability to bring diversity, relatability, and humor to the Star Wars universe while embodying the values of unity, friendship, and the importance of unsung heroes. Though he might not wield a lightsaber or pilot an X-wing, Klaud's impact is undeniable. In a galaxy defined by its diverse characters and stories, Klaud's presence enriches the tapestry of Star Wars, reminding us that every individual, no matter their role, can contribute to the fight for freedom and justice.
